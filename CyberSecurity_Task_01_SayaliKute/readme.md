# Part B: Password Security Review

## What Makes a Strong Password?

A strong password contains uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. It should be long, unique, and difficult to guess.

## Why Should Passwords Be Unique?

Unique passwords prevent attackers from accessing multiple accounts if one password becomes compromised.

## What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is an additional layer of security that requires more than one verification method before granting access to an account.

## Risks of Password Reuse

Using the same password across multiple accounts increases the risk of account compromise if one service experiences a data breach.

## Password Policy

* Use at least 12 characters.
* Include uppercase and lowercase letters.
* Include numbers and special characters.
* Avoid personal information.
* Enable MFA whenever possible.
* Use a unique password for each account.
* Change passwords if suspicious activity is detected.

# Part D: Cyber Threat Research

## Phishing

**Definition:**
A cyber attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information through fake emails, messages, or websites.

**Example:**
A fake banking email requesting login credentials.

**Prevention:**
Verify sender identity, avoid suspicious links, and enable MFA.

---

## Malware

**Definition:**
Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

**Example:**
Computer viruses and spyware.

**Prevention:**
Use antivirus software and avoid downloading files from untrusted sources.

---

## Ransomware

**Definition:**
A type of malware that encrypts files and demands payment for decryption.

**Example:**
WannaCry ransomware attack.

**Prevention:**
Maintain backups, update systems regularly, and avoid suspicious attachments.

---

## Social Engineering

**Definition:**
The manipulation of people to obtain confidential information.

**Example:**
An attacker pretending to be technical support staff.

**Prevention:**
Verify identities and never share sensitive information without confirmation.

---

## Data Breach

**Definition:**
Unauthorized access to confidential information.

**Example:**
Customer data leaked from a company database.

**Prevention:**
Use strong authentication, encryption, and security monitoring.
